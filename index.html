Colombia Unfiltered

From myth to reality. A country beyond Escobar.

About Susana Zapata

Susana Zapata is a passionate storyteller and proud Colombian who believes in changing the narrative of her country. For far too long, Colombia has been unjustly associated with drugs and the notorious figure of Pablo Escobar. But this is a story that is far from the truth. Colombia is a country of resilience, beauty, and diversity, full of history, culture, and hope. Through her work, Susana aims to break these misconceptions and show the world a Colombia beyond the clichés – a country that is blossoming, evolving, and redefining itself.

Voices in the Storm: Colombia’s Political Reality Today

Colombia’s political landscape is increasingly dynamic. Despite the challenges of a polarized society, there's a growing awareness and participation in politics, especially among young people. This engagement has led to significant strides in the fight for transparency and the reduction of corruption. Colombia’s political system, once marred by scandal and distrust, is gradually becoming more inclusive, with debates being stronger, more open, and inclusive of all segments of society.

Recent changes, such as the peace agreement with the FARC and efforts towards inclusion, have been major steps forward, although many obstacles remain. The young generation is determined to build a more transparent, accountable system. Despite the slow pace of reforms, democracy in Colombia is very much alive and evolving.

From Shadows to Light: Colombia in the 90s vs Now

The 90s were dark: bombings, cartels, fear. But Colombia rebuilt from within. Today, art, education and peace initiatives are the norm.

The Colombia of the 90s is not the same. While the memories of that dark time are still present in the country's collective consciousness, today, Colombia stands as a country that has learned from its mistakes, rebuilt itself and is now a symbol of resilience and hope in Latin America.

The Future of Colombia

The future of Colombia looks incredibly promising. With an increasing focus on sustainability, technology, and education, the country is stepping confidently into the 21st century. Colombian youth are leading the charge for social and economic reform, driven by innovation and the desire to change the narrative that once defined the country.

In the future, Colombia will continue to be a major player in Latin America, not just for its natural beauty but for its technological advances, social progress, and growing position as an international economic hub. The future is bright for Colombia as the country continues to embrace change and redefine itself as a global leader in peace, technology, and culture.

Bogotá

Colombia’s capital, **Bogotá**, is much more than just the political centre of the country. Situated at over 2,600 metres above sea level, the city is a melting pot of cultures and art. From the majestic **Monserrate**, with its panoramic views of the city, to the vibrant **La Candelaria**, the historical centre with its cobbled streets and colonial architecture, Bogotá invites you to explore its history while staying in touch with its modern life.

In addition to its historical architecture, Bogotá is home to important museums such as the **Gold Museum**, which holds one of the largest collections of pre-Columbian art, and the **Botero Museum**, featuring impressive works by the famous Colombian artist Fernando Botero. Don’t forget its gastronomy, which reflects a fusion of international and local influences, as well as its cultural offerings, with events like the **Ibero-American Theatre Festival**.

Medellín

**Medellín** was once a city known worldwide for its violence during the 80s and 90s. Today, it stands as an example of urban and social transformation. Known for its warm climate and friendly people, Medellín has evolved into a dynamic, vibrant city filled with innovation.

One of Medellín’s most iconic features is its **Metrocable**, a cable car system that connects the hillside neighbourhoods with the rest of the city, symbolising inclusion and progress. Medellín is also home to events such as the **Flower Festival**, a tribute to Antioquian culture, and the famous **Botero Plaza**, where sculptures by Fernando Botero breathe life into the city. This is a city that has managed to overcome its troubled past through education and culture.

Cali

**Cali**, the salsa capital, is undoubtedly one of the most cheerful and rhythmic cities in Colombia. Located on the banks of the **Cali River**, the city fills with music and colour during the **World Salsa Festival** and the **Cali Fair**, two of the city's most important events. But Cali is more than just salsa; it is a place where Afro-Colombian culture is celebrated, with a vibrant scene of dance, art, and theatre.

One of Cali’s most iconic landmarks is **Cristo Rey**, a giant statue of Christ that stands tall above the city, offering a spectacular view of the Cauca River valley. The city is also famous for its parks, such as **Parque de los Gatos** and **Parque del Perro**, which provide a break from the vibrant urban life.

Barranquilla

**Barranquilla** is the **Carnival capital**, and not just because it hosts one of the largest festivals in Colombia, but because this Caribbean city is a fusion of cultures, colours, and sounds. Its **Barranquilla Carnival** has been declared a **UNESCO World Heritage event**, and during this celebration, the streets are filled with parades, dances, music, and costumes.

The city is not only famous for its Carnival but also for its growth as a key industrial and commercial hub in the Caribbean region. **Barranquilla** has a strong cultural identity, reflected in both its music and architecture, with influences ranging from colonial times to art deco. **Calle 84** is a prime example of this fusion, with its nightlife and restaurants serving the best of coastal cuisine.

Bucaramanga

**Bucaramanga** is a city of contrasts, where the modern and the natural come together in harmony. Known as the **city of parks**, Bucaramanga boasts over 160 green areas, such as **Parque del Chicamocha**, which offers a breathtaking view of the **Chicamocha Canyon**, one of the most spectacular landscapes in Colombia.

In addition to its natural beauty, Bucaramanga is a city with a strong focus on education and innovation. It has become a development hub in northeastern Colombia, with modern infrastructure, high quality of life, and a friendly and welcoming population. Its architecture, which blends the classical and the modern, and its shopping malls and restaurants, make Bucaramanga a city that truly has everything to offer.
