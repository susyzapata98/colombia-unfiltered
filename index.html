Colombia Unfiltered

From myth to reality. A country beyond Escobar.

1. Voices in the Storm: Colombia’s Political Reality Today

Colombia’s political landscape is far from perfect, but it is not chaos. Tensions remain, but so does a growing, active civil society. Youth engagement is on the rise, debates are dynamic, and a demand for transparency is reshaping public life. Though challenges persist, democracy breathes and slowly evolves.

2. The Green Jewel of the Continent: Colombia’s Natural Beauty

Colombia offers rainforest, two oceans, Andean peaks, deserts, plains and rivers like Caño Cristales. As the second most biodiverse country on Earth, it is home to unique species of birds, butterflies and flora. Each region is a hidden treasure, rich in colour and life.

3. Liquid Gold: Colombian Coffee Around the World

Colombian coffee is more than a global product; it is a symbol of pride. Grown by families across the Coffee Region, it represents heritage and hard work. Colombia ranks third in exports but often first in quality, delivering warmth to tables worldwide.

4. Five Cities, Five Reasons to Believe

Bogotá – A cultural and intellectual hub with Monserrate’s view and Latin America’s largest bike network.
Medellín – Once violent, now a beacon of innovation with its Metrocable and inclusive urbanism.
Cali – The joyful heart of Afro-Colombian rhythm and the global capital of salsa.
Barranquilla – Home to the UNESCO-listed Carnival and a centre of modern development.
Bucaramanga – Framed by Chicamocha Canyon, it's a city blending nature and progress.

5. From Shadows to Light: Colombia in the 90s vs Now

The 1990s were Colombia’s darkest chapter: violence, bombings, cartels. But this isn’t the full story. Today, universities thrive, art flourishes, and former combatants rebuild. From survival to hope, Colombia has transformed its scars into canvases.

6. Colombia's Future: A Nation in Progress

Colombia’s future lies in innovation, peace, and identity. With tech start-ups, eco-tourism and community projects growing, the country is redefining its narrative. A younger, more connected generation is shaping a new path — one that looks forward, not back.
